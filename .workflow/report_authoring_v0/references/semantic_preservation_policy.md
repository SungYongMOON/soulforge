# Semantic-preservation policy

The protected semantic manifest records association-aware, multiplicity-aware
invariants for facts; predicates; signed numbers, precision, units, comparators,
ranges, tolerances, uncertainty and coverage basis; dates/times; locations;
identifiers; citations and visual anchors; table row/column/cell bindings;
candidate/comparator/metric/outcome/direction; negation; pronoun/coreference;
modality; attribution, roles and assurance; conditions, exclusions and scope;
causal/temporal relations; verdicts; and unconfirmed status.

Deterministic checks compare normalized protected fields by stable association and
verify that each declared surface form remains at its original target anchor. The
anchor and exact protected surface form are part of the baseline comparison for
every invariant, not only table cells. A bare set of tokens is insufficient:
duplicate occurrences and table coordinates are distinct. v0 has no executable
authorized-change contract, so protected movement, unit conversion, citation
renumbering, and protected-surface rewriting are rejected.
For every citation invariant, the reference entry id and object must agree, the
entry `source_ref` must remain one of that invariant's protected `evidence_refs`,
and the reader-facing reference label must expose the protected citation surface.
Changing only a reference entry to point at a different source is therefore a
blocked semantic change, not a cosmetic edit.
An identifier-shaped source citation such as a report or change-record id is
covered by its exact citation invariant for lexical intake; authors do not create
a duplicate identifier invariant solely because the lexical scanner also
classifies the same surface as an identifier. The exact surface, anchor,
reference-entry binding, and evidence refs remain protected.

These checks do not guarantee semantic equivalence. Success also requires a separate
semantic verifier whose actor and run identities differ from the final rewriter.
Its context identity must also differ, and its result must attest coverage of every
hash-bound approved input. It compares approved inputs with the final candidate,
not two projections of one normalized object. Do not give it the expected verdict
or the author transcript.

The summary derivation map binds every summary sentence to verified body claim IDs.
The reader projection excludes this map and all other audit records. The
`protected_semantic_manifest` artifact preserves the original protected baseline;
candidate inventory hashes and differences remain in the preservation audit.
Deterministic
checks cover exact schema, multiplicity, anchors, table coordinates, and normalized
protected values; the independent semantic verifier covers ambiguous role,
coreference, modality, causality, and meaning; a qualified human remains required
for publishable or production-ready use.
Before a publishable or production-ready claim, an independent human must inspect
owner-contract truth, claim-to-source fidelity, quantitative/table integrity,
reasoning, ambiguous semantic edits and pilot applicability. Any unresolved
difference blocks publication.
