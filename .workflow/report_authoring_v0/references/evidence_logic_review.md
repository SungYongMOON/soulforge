# Evidence-logic review

Keep report roles distinct and traceable:

- results present evidence;
- discussion interprets evidence and states limitations;
- conclusions follow from findings and the recorded reasoning path;
- recommendations follow from supported conclusions or causes and identify the
  action, responsible role and measurable intent where applicable;
- the executive summary is derived only after the body passes this review, each
  summary sentence points to verified body claim IDs, and no summary-only claim is
  added;
- repeated facts, tables, captions, conclusions and recommendations resolve to the
  same source association.

Do not promote temporal sequence to causality, reported material to laboratory
verification, or conditional/unconfirmed status to pass. If a logic review changes
a protected field, reopen technical-content review before polish.

Select the minimum role matrix and material optional roles from
`editorial_contract.md`. Reject repeated facts or placeholder prose used only to
populate an optional role. Check the
type-specific logic:

- experiment: named criteria, result/uncertainty bindings, tested-scope verdict;
- analysis: criteria and weights precede alternatives, comparable evidence and
  sensitivity support any recommendation;
- progress: plan-versus-actual uses a named baseline and keeps issue, risk,
  forecast, and aspiration distinct;
- presentation: BLUF/Ask is body-derived, evidence slides carry bounded takeaways,
  and material limits remain on the decision surface;
- other: roles follow the primary reader decision without empty generic sections.

Record why optional roles were included or omitted. A changed body claim
invalidates every dependent summary sentence and returns the document to summary
derivation after the body passes again.
